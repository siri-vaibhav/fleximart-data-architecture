## Limitations of Traditional RDBMS

Traditional relational database management systems (RDBMS) are well-suited for structured data and transactional workloads but face limitations when handling modern, large-scale applications. One major limitation is scalability. RDBMS systems primarily scale vertically, which involves upgrading hardware and can become expensive and inefficient for growing datasets.

Another limitation is rigid schema design. Any schema changes require migrations that may impact system availability and performance. This makes RDBMS less flexible when dealing with evolving data structures or semi-structured data.

RDBMS systems also struggle with handling massive volumes of unstructured or semi-structured data generated by modern applications, such as user reviews, logs, and social media data. Additionally, high join complexity can reduce performance when working with large datasets across multiple tables.

---

## Benefits of NoSQL Databases

NoSQL databases are designed to address the challenges of scalability, flexibility, and performance. One key benefit is horizontal scalability, allowing data to be distributed across multiple servers with ease. This makes NoSQL systems suitable for handling large volumes of data and high traffic workloads.

NoSQL databases offer flexible schema designs, enabling storage of semi-structured or unstructured data without predefined schemas. This flexibility allows faster development and easier adaptation to changing business requirements.

High availability and fault tolerance are additional advantages. Many NoSQL systems replicate data automatically across nodes, ensuring reliability even in case of failures. These features make NoSQL databases ideal for use cases such as real-time analytics, content management, and user-generated data.

---

## Trade-offs Between RDBMS and NoSQL

While NoSQL databases provide scalability and flexibility, they come with trade-offs. NoSQL systems often sacrifice strong consistency in favor of eventual consistency, which may not be suitable for all applications.

RDBMS systems provide strong ACID guarantees and are better suited for complex transactions and relational data integrity. On the other hand, NoSQL databases prioritize performance and scalability over strict consistency.

Choosing between RDBMS and NoSQL depends on the application requirements. Systems that require complex transactions benefit from RDBMS, while applications handling large-scale, distributed, or unstructured data benefit from NoSQL solutions.
